The Breadman is a 4-lane rhythm game inspired by Guitar Hero and Friday Night Funkin'. You play as Abraham - a real neighborhood bread delivery vendor - hitting notes to the beat as he makes his rounds through the block before sunrise.

## Features

- 5 original songs with escalating BPM (100-135 BPM)
- Hold notes, double-taps, and section-aware chart generation
- BREAD ZONE: hit a 25x combo to activate a x2 score multiplier with golden visual effects
- Dawn progression: the sky transitions from deep night to golden sunrise as you play
- 3 difficulty modes: Easy, Normal, Hard
- Level unlock progression - earn a B grade to unlock the next track
- "SHIFT COMPLETE" fanfare sequence with particle explosions
- Procedural synth engine as audio fallback (kick, snare, hihat, bass, chords, melody)
- 24 bread-themed loading tips and Abram's internal monologue quips
- Light/dark mode, mobile touch support, localStorage high scores

## The Tracklist

1. **Bread Man Blues** (100 BPM) - The original. Chill funk groove.
2. **The Morning Route** (105 BPM) - Easy cruising through the neighborhood.
3. **Rush Hour** (120 BPM) - Traffic picks up. So does the tempo.
4. **Double Parked** (135 BPM) - The fastest track. Dave the parking officer has no mercy.
5. **Legend of the Block** (125 BPM) - The finale. Celebratory soul anthem.

## The Story

Jeff's neighbor Abram Pure is a bread delivery vendor who starts his route at 2 AM. Jeff wrote a song about him called "Abraham the Breadman" and built this game to go with it. Every quip, every loading tip, every mechanic is pulled from real bread route life.

Built as a single HTML file with zero dependencies. Canvas rendering, Web Audio API, and pure JavaScript.
