PHARMAGEDDON is a fast-paced browser game where you play as a retail pharmacist surviving 10 increasingly chaotic shifts. Take prescriptions, fill them, verify for errors, answer phones, manage drive-thru, and keep your stress from boiling over - all while corporate keeps adding to your plate.

It's authentic. It's funny. And if you've ever worked behind a pharmacy counter, it'll hit close to home.

## Features

- **10 Shifts** - each with a unique name and escalating difficulty
- **26 Real Medications** - from Lisinopril to Cyclobenzaprine, all with accurate dose ranges
- **4 Meters** - Stress, Safety, Corporate Satisfaction, and Community Reputation
- **24 Achievements** - including "Cried in Honda Civic" and "Denied for Albuterol"
- **Co-Op Mode** - two players, one keyboard: Tech handles customers, Pharmacist handles fills
- **Upgrade Shop** - spend score points on permanent improvements between shifts
- **Zero Dependencies** - pure HTML/CSS/JavaScript, single file, runs anywhere

## Why This Game Exists

Dr. Jeff Bullock spent years behind the pharmacy counter before becoming a technology CEO. This game captures the real chaos of retail pharmacy - the insurance denials, the understaffing, the impossible expectations, and the patients who make it all worth it.

It's not cynical. It's a love letter to every pharmacist who has survived the shift.

## Technical Details

- Built with vanilla JavaScript, zero framework overhead
- Web Audio API for all sound effects (no audio files)
- Cloud score sync via REST API
- Fully responsive with mobile-optimized controls
- PWA-ready with installable manifest
