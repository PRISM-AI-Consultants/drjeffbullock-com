# Design Context — drjeffbullock.com

## Target audience
Speaking bookers / event organizers, enterprise & SMB prospects evaluating PRISM/VersAssist, media and journalists, potential partners and investors, and people discovering Dr. Jeff Bullock's work (books, research, the two dreams). They arrive curious about a credible, unusual figure: a PharmD who became an AI builder and is chasing two enormous missions.

## Use cases
- Understand who Jeff is and why he's credible in under 10 seconds.
- Grasp the two dreams (make giving go viral; solve poverty for everyone) and feel their weight.
- Book him to speak / contact him.
- Explore the proof: PRISM, VersAssist, books, research, projects, media, VR.

## Brand personality / tone
**Authoritative, visionary, editorial — with soul.** A serious thought leader, not a corporate consultant and not a generic personal-brand template. Confident and intellectual (research-backed, PharmD rigor) but mission-driven and human (faith, community, Black wealth, generosity). Should feel like the brand of someone with a big, almost audacious vision who has the receipts to back it. Distinctive and memorable; passes the "how was this made?" test.

## Deliberately its OWN identity
Must NOT look like the Daily Impact app (that is warm devotional "Daily Light" — paper, gold, Fraunces). This site is a different project and gets a different, distinct aesthetic. Same rigorous design process, separate visual world.

## Hard constraints
- Next.js 15 App Router + React + Tailwind. No framework change.
- Works in BOTH light and dark (site has a ThemeToggle).
- Keep existing routes/nav/content structure and the two-dreams section.
- Preserve real content: name, **PharmD** credential (never PhD), the two-dreams copy, CTAs "See the mission" / "Book Me to Speak", speaking-at logos, projects/proof.
- No em-dashes / en-dashes in copy. Accessible (WCAG AA contrast, focus states, reduced-motion).
- Credibility is paramount: nothing gimmicky that undercuts trust.
